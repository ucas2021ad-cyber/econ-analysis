# ⚡ Econ Analysis – Energy & Policy

### Nunavut Energy System Analysis & Sankey Validation

## 📌 Overview

This repository presents an economic and policy analysis of Nunavut’s energy system, with a focus on structural dependency, data validation, and policy implications.

The project combines:

* Policy analysis
* Data validation
* Energy flow visualization (Sankey diagrams)

to understand how energy systems operate in remote, resource-constrained regions.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze Nunavut’s energy structure and policy environment
* Validate energy flow data using Sankey diagrams
* Identify inconsistencies between modeled and official data
* Evaluate economic and policy implications of energy dependency

---

## 📊 Key Components

### 1. Energy Policy Analysis

* Examines Nunavut’s heavy reliance on **diesel imports**
* Highlights:

  * Lack of energy diversification
  * Infrastructure constraints
  * High energy costs and vulnerability
* Provides **policy recommendations** for sustainable transition

---

### 2. Sankey Diagram Validation

* Compares modeled energy flows with official data (CER)
* Identifies discrepancies in:

  * Transportation energy use
  * Industrial consumption
  * Petroleum dependency
* Explains differences due to:

  * Data year mismatch
  * Modeling assumptions
  * Aggregation vs disaggregation

---

### 3. Data Analysis (Excel)

* Structured dataset for energy flows and sector usage
* Used for:

  * Cross-validation
  * Quantitative comparison
  * Supporting Sankey construction

---

### 4. Energy & Carbon Flow Visualizations

* Sankey diagrams for:

  * Energy flow
  * Carbon flow
* Used to:

  * Simplify complex systems
  * Highlight dependency on a single energy source
  * Support policy interpretation

---

## 📈 Key Insights

* **Extreme energy dependency**
  → Nunavut relies almost entirely on imported diesel

* **High economic vulnerability**
  → Energy costs driven by transportation and infrastructure limits

* **Policy blind spots**
  → Limited structural transition despite awareness of issues

* **Data interpretation matters**
  → Modeling assumptions significantly affect conclusions
